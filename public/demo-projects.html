Step 5 — Kanban Board. Six columns — Backlog, To Do, In Progress, Review, Done, and Blocked — give a full task workflow. Each card shows the phase colour stripe, title, priority badge, and due date. Drag and drop any card between columns and the status updates instantly. Click the plus icon in any column header to add a new task with phase, priority, due date, and estimated hours.

Step 6 — Workflow Canvas. The workflow canvas lets you visually design your A-I pipeline — similar to n8n or Visio. Drag node types from the palette onto the canvas, then draw edges between output and input ports by dragging from one node to another. Pan with click-and-drag on the background, zoom with the scroll wheel, and changes auto-save every one-and-a-half seconds.

Step 8 — n8n Webhook Automation. Connect any n8n workflow to project events — phase completions, milestone achievements, task completions, risk threshold breaches, or experiment logs. Simply paste the n8n webhook U-R-L, choose the trigger event, and hit Test to verify the connection. Automations fire silently in the background every time the event occurs.
